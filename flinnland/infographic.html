History of Finnish Architecture

Finnish architecture begins with the Uralic peoples, who migrated out of the Kama River valley and settled in modern Russia, Fennoscandia, the Baltics, and Hungary. Only loosely linked tribes, the Finns were crusaded by Sweden and annexed in 1250, then lost to Russia in 1809, gaining architectural inspiration from both countries. Finnish independence in 1917 allowed buildings to reflect a newfound national pride which gave way to sustainable building practices that still define Finnish architecture to this day.
